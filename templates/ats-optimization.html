What Is ATS and How Can You Pass It?

Intro:
Ever apply for a job and never hear back? The culprit could be an ATS — Applicant Tracking System. These bots screen resumes before a recruiter even sees them. Here’s how to make your resume ATS-friendly in 2025.

What Is an ATS?
An ATS is software used by employers to scan resumes for keywords and rank candidates based on relevance. If your resume doesn’t include the right terms or uses a bad format, it might get rejected automatically.

Common ATS Traps:

  • Fancy designs with images, columns, or charts
  • Uncommon fonts or symbols
  • Using headers like "Professional Story" instead of "Experience"
  • Saving your resume as an image or incompatible file type

How to Beat the ATS:

  • Use standard section headers like "Experience," "Education," "Skills"
  • Stick to .docx or clean PDFs
  • Match your wording to the job posting (especially job titles and key skills)
  • Avoid text in tables or graphics — they can’t be read

ATS-Friendly Resume Checklist:

  • ✅ Simple, clean layout
  • ✅ Job-specific keywords included
  • ✅ Accurate job titles and formatting
  • ✅ No photos, icons, or graphics
  • ✅ Proper file type (.docx or .pdf)
