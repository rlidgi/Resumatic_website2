Power Words to Use in Your Resume (And Ones to Avoid)

Intro:
Hiring managers scan resumes in under 10 seconds. Strong action verbs make your resume pop — weak, generic phrases do the opposite. This guide helps you identify power words that showcase impact and leadership.

25 Resume Power Words That Impress:
Achieved Boosted Created Delivered Designed Developed Drove Enhanced Executed Generated Improved Increased Launched Led Managed Negotiated Optimized Orchestrated Oversaw Planned Reduced Spearheaded Streamlined Trained Upgraded

10 Resume Words to Avoid:
Responsible for Hard-working Go-getter Results-oriented Team player Detail-oriented Fast learner Excellent communication skills Self-starter Motivated

Why Power Words Work:
They show ownership and outcome. Instead of saying you “were responsible for” something, power verbs clarify what you did and what result you achieved.

Before and After Example:
❌ "Responsible for social media"
✅ "Led and executed a social media strategy, increasing engagement by 35% in 3 months"

How to Choose the Right Verbs:
Match verbs to the skills in the job description. For example, if a posting emphasizes project leadership, use verbs like “managed,” “orchestrated,” or “directed.” If it's an analytical role, try “analyzed,” “evaluated,” or “synthesized.”
